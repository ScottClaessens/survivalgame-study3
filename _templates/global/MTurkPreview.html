Title of the study: Group decision making

Length of the study: about 30 minutes

Brief description: LIVE decision making experiment with one other participant

Payment: average $3.00 ($1.00 plus bonuses)

  1. An initial participation payment ($1.00)
  2. Answering the comprehension questions ($0.02 per question)
  3. Waiting to be connected to a partner ($0.05 per minute)
  4. Playing the game ($1.85 on average)
  5. Answers to optional post-game questions ($0.01 per question)

Consent form

I am a visiting scholar in the Department of Psychology at Arizona State University. I am conducting a research study to understand how people make decisions in groups.

I am inviting your participation, which will involve playing a live group game with another real player. You will first provide some basic demographic information. You will then read some instructions about the live group game. Before beginning the game, you will play an example round with the computer and answer some comprehension questions to make sure you understand the game. The game itself will require you to manage a resource over time. The study should take no longer than 30 minutes, and you have the right not to answer any question, and to stop participation at any time.

Your participation in this study is voluntary. If you choose not to participate or to withdraw from the study at any time, there will be no penalty. You must be 18 or older to participate in this study.

There are five ways of being paid in this study. The first initial payment of $1.00 is paid to every participant when they begin the study. An extra $0.02 will be paid for each comprehension question answered. Participants will be paid $0.05 per minute spent waiting to be paired with another player. The game itself pays according to the participant's performance (average $1.85). Finally, an extra $0.01 will be paid for each optional post-game question answered. We expect players to earn an average of $3.00 in total ($1.14 - $4.00).

There are no foreseeable risks or discomforts to your participation.

Your responses will be anonymous. The results of this study may be used in reports, presentations, or publications but your name will never be used.

If you have any questions concerning the research study, please contact the research team at: sclaess1@asu.edu or aktipis@asu.edu.

If you have any questions about your rights as a subject/participant in this research, or if you feel you have been placed at risk, you can contact the Chair of the Human Subjects Institutional Review Board, through the ASU Office of Research Integrity and Assurance, at (480) 965-6788.
